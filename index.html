Welcome to Hangout Tours, where you can visit the streets of almost any city or locale and be led by knowledgeable guides, from anywhere in the world!

As an enhancement for Google Hangouts and Google Maps, all you need is a Google account to get started immediately. Start a regular Google Hangout, and open the Hangout Tour app. Once you accept a few settings changes, a map and streetview image will appear and then you’re ready to go! Now it’s not just you and Pegman walking the streets, but you and an experienced guide teaching you all you need to know.

Organize a tour with your friends or colleagues, or hop in to any of the open tours scheduled already. Never leave for a new place without knowing everything a true native should. Never limit learning about a city because of physical boundaries. The world is waiting, so start a Hangout Tour today!

Jacksonville, FL

"The River City"

After a complex history of a Spanish colony turning into the American frontier, Jacksonville has situated itself as one of the nation's largest cities. While often overlooked because of in-state neighbors like Orlanda and Miami, Jacksonville truly ranks as a complete city by providing quality destinations and weather to its 800,000 residents.

Today’s Hangout Tour: Teach for America

In 2007, community leaders in Jacksonville sought out Teach For America as a vital part of the solution to improve educational prospects for students in Duval County. Since Teach For America – Jacksonville launched with 50 corps members in 2008, we have brought over 200 high quality teachers to Jacksonville who have directly impacted the lives of over 13,000 low-income and minority students across Duval County. Because of successes like this we are building towards a scale that will allow us to effect transformational change across the entire city.

Memphis, TN

"The Home of Soul"

Widely considered the home of rock n' roll, gospel, and soul music, it's hard to imagine a better sounding city than Memphis. But the appeal goes way beyond music history, to some of the nation's premiere concert venues, and a massive outdoor show called Memphis in May. Need something other than music? Memphis is a true city with art museums, a world class zoo, historical sights, and more

Today’s Hangout Tour: Teach for America

Nearly 50 years after Dr. King gave his life here in the fight for equality, only 4 percent of kids in our city graduate high school ready for college. Only 26 percent of residents in the Memphis Metropolitan Area hold a bachelor’s degree. We are not living up to the legacy left to us by Civil Rights leaders when a quality education is still a privilege in our region and not a right. As we consider the educational landscape in Memphis today, we are convinced that our city must be the place that fulfills Dr. King’s dreams of a day when we are “an oasis of freedom and justice”.

Nashville, TN

"Music City, USA"

Nashville faces tough competition to the claim of "Music City", but it certainly packs a punch in that fued. From the Grand Old Opry to the Country Music Hall of Fame, the state capital of Tennessee has no shortage of musical expertise. Around the city, you'll also find major sports franchises, outdoor festivals, and historical museums.

Today’s Hangout Tour: Teach for America

Charlotte’s education system has a rich history, from being at the forefront of the desegregation movement in the 1970s to winning the prestigious Broad Prize for Urban Education in 2011. Despite this, there is still a very real disparity in educational opportunities between low-income students and their more affluent peers. Within a few miles of the prospering business districts of downtown, there are schools where only one-third of students are on grade level in reading and math. Teach For America is working with school, community, political, and business leaders across Charlotte to change this.

Charlotte, NC

"A Hornet's Nest of Rebellion"

Charlotte is the largest city in North Carolina, and has long lived up to the standard of its royal namesake. After its founding in 1755, Charlotte was famed for its contributions to the rebellions of the American Revolution and Civil War. It has since turned this tenacious spirit into a thriving, and still growing, metropolis.
